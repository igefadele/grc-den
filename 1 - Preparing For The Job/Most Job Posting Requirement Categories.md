## Job Posting Requirements Categories
Over the years in my career, I've come across different job postings and many requirements. Many roles split cleanly into two high-leverage categories, especially now that AI-assisted software development has become a big thing:

### A. Core GRC & Framework Specialists (Standard Chartered, Enterprise Teams)

#### The Focus: 
Designing, implementing, and running systematic risk management assessments across business units.

#### Key Requirements: 
* Fluency in industry-standard frameworks: ISO/IEC 27001 (ISMS), NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF), COBIT, and CIS Controls.

* The ability to map abstract regulatory rules to actual data handling processes.

* Writing clear, enforceable policies and Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs).


### B. Technical & AI Security Auditors (The Tech-Forward Teams)

#### The Focus: 
Securing modern SaaS, cloud-native deployments, and managing risks introduced by Generative AI tools.

#### Key Requirements:

* Product Security Assessments: Verifying technical mitigations for application flaws (like the OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities).

* Data Protection & Sovereignty: Ensuring customer records adhere to strict regional boundaries (GDPR, cross-border regulations).

* AI Governance: Assessing input/output pipeline sanitation, mitigating prompt injection, and preventing data leakage through public LLM endpoints.

## Unfair Advantage For Experienced Software Engineers (The 0.1% Positioning)

Seriously, people with technical background has an unfair advantage as a GRC. So if you are one, then, swim in advantage Bro/Sis.

Most applicants entering GRC from audit, business, or legal backgrounds can only verify compliance after the fact by asking developers for manual logs.

* As a Software Engineer, you look at compliance as a technical engineering constraint. Your narrative shifts from manual checking to automated, continuous enforcement:

* You don't just write an access control policy: You know how to audit Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) in a cloud environment and configure Least Privilege using short-lived tokens.

* You don't just audit supply-chain risk: You deploy automated dependency tracking tools (like Snyk or Dependabot) straight into git staging pipelines to block vulnerable dependencies automatically.

* You don't just read an AI policy: You can look at the data flow of a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) system or an n8n agentic workflow and identify exactly where data leakage or prompt-injection boundaries are broken.
