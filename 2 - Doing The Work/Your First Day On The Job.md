# Your First Day on the Job (Step-by-Step)

Congratulation Chief, you now have the job.

I will assume the company you are hired into is the amazing [Saviliate](https://saviliate.com).

So, let's say you are the new Technical GRC Analyst or The Information Security Engineer at [Saviliate](https://saviliate.com). And you are now resuming for your first day at work in the role.

Your first day should focus on systematic evaluation rather than writing immediate fixes.

Your first day should look like this:

### 09:00 - 11:00 | System Onboarding & Access Provisioning: 

You set up your corporate identity credentials. You ensure your account uses multi-factor authentication (MFA) from the start, confirming that the security team practices the access control policies they document.

### 11:00 - 13:00 | Architecture Review & Reading Existing SOPs: 

You request the team's current application topology charts and security documentation. You read their existing Incident Response plans, Data Privacy guidelines, and Change Management policies to map out their current operational baseline.

### 13:00 - 15:00 | Technical Infrastructure Discovery: 

You gain read-only visibility into the code repositories and cloud administration environments. You look closely at active GitHub branch protection rules, verify where database environmental variables are stored, and inspect how access logging is set up.

### 15:00 - 17:00 | Initial Gap Analysis & Defining the Next Steps: 

You close out your first day by listing any immediate compliance vulnerabilities or operational gaps discovered between their written policies and active technical setups. You save this overview as the roadmap for your upcoming 30-day security optimization strategy.
